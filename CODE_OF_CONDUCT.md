# Code of Conduct

Athena is an open-source project under the BSD-3-Clause license. We expect
everyone who interacts with the project — in issues, pull requests, comments,
or any other channel — to follow a small set of basic community norms.

## Our pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as
contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in Athena a
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Focusing on what is best for the community.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully.
- Putting the community's interests above one's own when relevant.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- The use of sexualised language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or
  advances.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards
of acceptable behaviour. Maintainers will take appropriate and fair corrective
action in response to any instances of unacceptable behaviour.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned with this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently
any contributor for other behaviours that they deem inappropriate, threatening,
offensive, or harmful.

## Reporting concerns

To report a Code of Conduct concern confidentially, email
[research@villmow.us](mailto:research@villmow.us) with the subject `Athena Code of Conduct`.
Please do not open a public issue. Maintainers will restrict access to the report, acknowledge it,
and communicate the next steps directly with the reporter when contact information is available.
This route is for conduct concerns; report security vulnerabilities through the separate process in
[`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md).

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — repositories, issue
trackers, discussions, and any other communication channel used by the project.
It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public
spaces (e.g., using an official project e-mail address, posting via an
official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an
online or offline event).
